Breaking News

7th Agricultural Census Launched

The Chief Minister, Mir Sarfraz Bugti, inaugurated the 7th Agricultural Census in Balochistan. It’s an Integrated Digital Count using GIS technology for more accurate agriculture & livestock data.

Source: The Nation

Organic Agriculture Policy 2024 Approved

The Balochistan government approved the Organic Agriculture Policy 2024 to promote sustainable farming and support 12,000 farmers across 95,000 acres. Key districts include Barkhan, Lasbela, Kohlu, and Khuzdar.

Sources: Dawn, UrduPoint

Promotion of Oilseed Crops

The Secretary of Agriculture emphasized promoting oilseed crops and improving plant protection measures. Awareness campaigns are being carried out by the Agriculture Extension Department.

Source: UrduPoint

Restoration of Flood-Damaged Water Channels

Water channels damaged during the 2022 floods have been rehabilitated. This will benefit around 57,000 acres of farmland and significantly boost annual production.

Source: SAMAA TV

Modernization of Farming & Budget Increase

The government is introducing modern farming techniques and has raised the agriculture budget by 36% to improve inputs, market access, and reduce middlemen.

Source: INP News

Livestock Incentives

Plans include soft loans for youth to start livestock projects, mobile veterinary teams across 36 districts, upgrading veterinary hospitals, and a training institute in Turbat.

Source: INP News

ABOUT OUR MISSION

Agriculture MISSION

The mission of the Agriculture Department, Government of Balochistan, is to enhance agricultural productivity, ensure food security, and improve the livelihoods of farming communities. The department is committed to promoting sustainable farming practices, introducing modern technologies, conserving natural resources, and supporting research and innovation to make agriculture more resilient and competitive.

About our Vision

Our agricultural Vision

To build a prosperous, food-secure, and sustainable Balochistan where modern agriculture and cooperative systems empower farmers, protect natural resources, and contribute significantly to the province’s economy and well-being.

About our Plan

Our Plan

The current plan of the Agriculture Department, Government of Balochistan, is centered on modernizing the agriculture sector and making it more sustainable. The department is working to expand irrigation systems, restore flood-damaged water channels, and implement the Organic Agriculture Policy 2024 to promote safe and eco-friendly farming. Efforts are being made to strengthen agricultural research and extension services, encourage the cultivation of oilseed crops, fruits, and vegetables, and provide incentives and training for youth and farmers to adopt innovative practices. Through the introduction of digital agricultural census systems, accurate data is being collected to support better planning and decision-making. In addition, livestock development is being prioritized with veterinary services, soft loans, and new training facilities. Together, these initiatives aim to boost productivity, raise farmer incomes, ensure food security, and position agriculture as a key driver of Balochistan’s economic growth.
